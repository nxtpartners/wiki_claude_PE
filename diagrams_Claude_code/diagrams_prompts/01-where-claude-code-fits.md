# Diagram 1: Where Claude Code fits

Diagram generation prompt (for Gemini or ChatGPT). Output image: `cc-where-it-fits.png`

---

Design a genuinely well made diagram that places Claude Code alongside claude.ai for a private equity teaching guide. It shows the reader one thing: these are two complementary ways to work with the same Claude. This is a client-facing concept graphic for KPMG Private Equity professionals, sitting inside a light, premium, institutional web page. Aim for world-class information design, the kind of orienting exhibit a senior training deck would put on one page. Calm, senior, precise, and clearly made on purpose.

**The concept to visualize:**

Two complementary ways to work with the same Claude, shown side by side as a matched pair.

- One side is claude.ai, the chat app in your browser. You paste or upload copies of documents and have a conversation. It is best for questions, analysis, and drafting. This is where most people start.
- The other side is Claude Code, Claude working directly inside the files and folders on your computer (in an editor like VS Code). It can read a whole folder, edit files in place, and run multi-step tasks, asking your approval before each change. It is best for hands-on, file-heavy work, and it is what you add next.

The core idea to land, expressed clearly somewhere: it is the same Claude, just two ways to work. The two sides should feel like siblings, matched and balanced, not a hierarchy or an upgrade path where one replaces the other.

**Required text labels (use these exactly, correctly spelled):**
- The two names, verbatim: "claude.ai" and "Claude Code".
- The one-line descriptions, verbatim: "Chat in your browser" and "Works in your files on your computer".
- The best-for lines, verbatim: "Best for questions, analysis, drafting" and "Best for hands-on, file-heavy work".
- The sequence cues, verbatim: "Start here" (with claude.ai) and "Add this next" (with Claude Code).
- The unifying line, verbatim: "Same Claude, two ways to work."

**Your creative freedom:**

You own the design. Decide the structure, the composition, the arrangement of the two sides, the container shapes, and the visual metaphor for "same Claude, two ways." Set them side by side, mirror them across a central spine, or use any arrangement you judge clearest and most elegant, as long as three things read at a glance: which side is claude.ai and which is Claude Code, what each is best for, and the fact that they are the same Claude expressed two ways. Do not feel bound to any particular arrangement of columns, panels, connectors, or a center callout. Let claude.ai read as the familiar browser starting point and Claude Code read as the hands-on, in-your-files companion you add next. Make it beautiful and make it clear.

**Canvas and brand:**
- Light or white canvas (`#FFFFFF` or a very light tone). High resolution. Generous margins and quiet negative space.
- Brand palette, blue-led: deep blue `#00338D`, cobalt `#1E49E2`, sky `#ACEAFF`, teal `#00A3A1`, confidential red `#B42318`, ink `#0B1B33`, muted ink `#5A6678`, hairline `#E3E8EF`, white `#FFFFFF`, soft tint `#F5F7FA`. Lead with blue and ink. This is not a decision diagram, so use teal and red sparingly if at all.
- Typography: Inter or a near-identical clean grotesque. Real, legible, correctly spelled US English. Keep labels short.
- Depth is welcome and tasteful: soft shadow, subtle layering, gentle in-palette gradients, well-made containers. Never gaudy. Reference bar: world-class information design in the spirit of FT and Bloomberg graphics, Pentagram, and Stripe Press.

**Overall feel:**

A calm, balanced orienting graphic: two matched ways to work rendered with real craft in every label and container. The reader should instantly understand that claude.ai and Claude Code are the same Claude, that you start on claude.ai, and that you add Claude Code for hands-on, file-heavy work.

**Avoid:** dark or navy backgrounds, neon, purple or violet, sci-fi or circuit-board motifs, hexagon tech patterns, emoji or clip-art stock icons, childish or generic "AI infographic" looks, busy or noisy backgrounds, watermarks, and any misspelled or lorem text.
