# Diagram 3: The approval loop

Diagram generation prompt (for Gemini or ChatGPT). Output image: `cc-approval-loop.png`

---

Design a genuinely well made diagram of a repeating approval loop for a private equity teaching guide. It shows the reader one thing: working with Claude Code is a cycle you run, and nothing changes on your computer without your approval. This is a client-facing concept graphic for KPMG Private Equity professionals, sitting inside a light, premium, institutional web page. Aim for world-class information design, the kind of process exhibit a senior training deck would put on one page. Calm, senior, precise, and clearly made on purpose.

**The concept to visualize:**

A repeating four-move cycle that returns to its start so the reader understands it is meant to be run again. It is not a one-way pipeline: after the fourth move, the loop comes back around to the first. The reader should also grasp the point of the loop: nothing changes on your computer without your approval, and you run this loop until the work is right.

**The four moves (in this order, then back to the start):**
1. You ask: "Describe the task in plain English."
2. Claude plans: "It proposes a clear, step-by-step plan."
3. You approve: "Say yes, or send it back to adjust."
4. It acts, you verify: "Claude makes the change; you check it against the source."

After "It acts, you verify," the loop returns to "You ask." That closing return is essential to the meaning.

**Required text labels (use these exactly, correctly spelled):**
- The four move titles, verbatim: "You ask", "Claude plans", "You approve", "It acts, you verify".
- Each move's one-line description, verbatim as written above.
- The core idea, expressed clearly somewhere in the composition, verbatim: "Nothing changes without your yes."

**Your creative freedom:**

You own the design. Decide the structure, the composition, the flow direction, the node shapes, and the visual metaphor for the cycle. Render it as a circle, a ring, a loop of any looping form, or any arrangement you judge clearest and most elegant, as long as the four moves read in order and the cycle visibly returns to its start. Do not feel bound to any particular arrangement of stations, positions, arcs, connectors, or a center callout. Make the looping, iterative nature instantly legible: the reader should see at a glance that this is a repeating habit you run until the work is right, and that the "you approve" gate is what keeps you in control. Make it beautiful and make it clear.

**Canvas and brand:**
- Light or white canvas (`#FFFFFF` or a very light tone). High resolution. Generous margins and quiet negative space.
- Brand palette, blue-led: deep blue `#00338D`, cobalt `#1E49E2`, sky `#ACEAFF`, teal `#00A3A1`, confidential red `#B42318`, ink `#0B1B33`, muted ink `#5A6678`, hairline `#E3E8EF`, white `#FFFFFF`, soft tint `#F5F7FA`. Lead with blue and ink. You may use a touch of teal to mark the "you approve" moment as the positive gate; otherwise use teal and red sparingly if at all.
- Typography: Inter or a near-identical clean grotesque. Real, legible, correctly spelled US English. Keep labels short.
- Depth is welcome and tasteful: soft shadow, subtle layering, gentle in-palette gradients, well-made containers. Never gaudy. Reference bar: world-class information design in the spirit of FT and Bloomberg graphics, Pentagram, and Stripe Press.

**Overall feel:**

A calm, exact, obviously iterative process diagram: dimensional and considered, with real craft in every label and connector. The reader should instantly understand that working with Claude Code is a loop you run until it is right, and that nothing changes without your yes.

**Avoid:** dark or navy backgrounds, neon, purple or violet, sci-fi or circuit-board motifs, hexagon tech patterns, emoji or clip-art stock icons, childish or generic "AI infographic" looks, busy or noisy backgrounds, watermarks, and any misspelled or lorem text.
