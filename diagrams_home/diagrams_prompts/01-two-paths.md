# Diagram: One Claude, two ways to work (homepage hero)

Diagram generation prompt (for Gemini, ChatGPT, or nano-banana). Output image: `home-two-paths.png`

---

Design a genuinely well made hero diagram for the homepage of a private equity teaching guide. It shows the reader one idea at a glance: there is one Claude, and there are two complementary ways to put it to work on their deal work. This is a client-facing concept graphic for KPMG Private Equity professionals, sitting at the top of a light, premium, institutional web page. Aim for world-class information design, the kind of orienting exhibit a senior training deck would lead with. Calm, senior, precise, and clearly made on purpose.

**The concept to visualize:**

A single flow that moves from one starting point, through two parallel paths, to one shared destination.

- It begins with the reader's own private equity work: the deal documents they already have (information memoranda, financial models, memos, data-room exports). This is the one input everything flows from.
- From there it opens into two parallel, equally weighted paths, the two ways to work with the same Claude:
  - **claude.ai**, the chat app in the browser. You paste or upload copies of documents and have a conversation. Best for questions, analysis, and drafting. This is where most people start.
  - **Claude Code**, Claude working directly inside the files and folders on your computer, in an editor. It reads whole folders, edits files in place, and runs multi-step tasks, asking approval before each change. Best for hands-on, file-heavy work. This is what you add next.
- Both paths arrive at the same destination: a confident, verified result that stays confidential. The two paths converge here, reinforcing that they lead to the same quality of outcome, kept private throughout.

The single most important idea to land, expressed clearly somewhere: it is one Claude, offered two ways to work, and both lead to the same trustworthy, confidential result. The two paths should feel like balanced siblings, not a hierarchy or an upgrade where one replaces the other. claude.ai reads as the familiar starting point; Claude Code reads as the hands-on companion you add next.

**Required text labels (use these exactly, correctly spelled US English):**
- The unifying line, verbatim: "One Claude, two ways to work."
- The starting point, verbatim: "Your PE work" with a short supporting note, verbatim: "CIMs, models, memos, data rooms".
- The two names, verbatim: "claude.ai" and "Claude Code".
- The one-line descriptions, verbatim: "Chat in your browser" and "Works in your files on your computer".
- The best-for lines, verbatim: "Best for questions, analysis, drafting" and "Best for hands-on, file-heavy work".
- The track and sequence cues, verbatim: "Core Track, start here" (with claude.ai) and "Advanced Track, add next" (with Claude Code).
- The shared destination, verbatim: "Confident, verified output" and "Confidential by design".

Keep labels short and let some be optional supporting notes if the composition is cleaner with fewer words, but the two names, the two descriptions, the unifying line, and the shared destination must all be present and legible.

**Your creative freedom:**

You own the design. Decide the whole composition: the direction of the flow (a left-to-right journey, a branching spine, a converging path, or any arrangement you judge clearest), the shapes of the containers, the way the two paths separate and rejoin, and the visual metaphor for "one Claude, two ways." Nothing here prescribes geometry, columns, connector styles, or exact positions. The only fixed requirements are the meaning, the verbatim labels, and the brand palette. Make three things read instantly: that everything starts from the reader's own PE work, that there are two balanced ways to work (clearly labelled claude.ai and Claude Code), and that both arrive at the same confident, confidential result. Make it beautiful and make it clear.

**Canvas and brand:**
- Light or white canvas (`#FFFFFF` or a very light tone). High resolution. A wide, landscape composition suited to sitting at the top of a web page, with generous margins and quiet negative space.
- Brand palette, blue-led: deep blue `#00338D`, cobalt `#1E49E2`, sky `#ACEAFF`, teal `#00A3A1`, confidential red `#B42318`, ink `#0B1B33`, muted ink `#5A6678`, hairline `#E3E8EF`, white `#FFFFFF`, soft tint `#F5F7FA`. Lead with deep blue and ink; let cobalt carry the flow lines and motion. Reserve teal for the positive final outcome (the confident, verified result). Use red only if a small confidentiality cue genuinely helps; it is not required.
- Typography: Inter or a near-identical clean grotesque. A confident, heavier weight for titles and the two names, a comfortable readable weight for descriptions and notes, eyebrows in uppercase with generous letter spacing. Real, legible, correctly spelled US English only.
- Depth is welcome and tasteful: soft, low shadows to lift key elements, subtle layering so the eye reads what sits in front, gentle in-palette gradients, and well made containers where they aid clarity. Dimensional and designed, never flat, never gaudy. Reference bar: world-class information design in the spirit of Financial Times and Bloomberg graphics, Pentagram, and Stripe Press.

**Overall feel:**

A calm, balanced orienting graphic that a senior partner would be happy to open a training with: one starting point, two matched ways to work, one confident and confidential result, rendered with real craft in every label and container. The reader should understand in a glance that it is the same Claude, that you start on claude.ai, that you add Claude Code for hands-on work, and that either way the result is trustworthy and private.

**Avoid:** dark or navy backgrounds, neon, purple or violet, sci-fi or circuit-board motifs, hexagon tech patterns, emoji or clip-art stock icons, childish or generic "AI infographic" looks, busy or noisy backgrounds, watermarks, and any misspelled or lorem text.
