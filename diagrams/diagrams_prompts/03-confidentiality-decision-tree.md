# Diagram 3: The confidentiality decision tree

Diagram generation prompt (for Gemini or ChatGPT).

---

Design a genuinely well made decision tree for a private equity teaching guide. It answers one question for the reader: can this document go into Claude? This is a client-facing concept graphic for KPMG Private Equity professionals, sitting inside a light, premium, institutional web page. Aim for world-class information design, the kind of decision exhibit a senior compliance team would hand to a partner. Calm, senior, precise, and clearly made on purpose.

**The decision to visualize:**

The reader is deciding whether a document is safe to put into Claude. The rule is an AND-chain: ALL five of the checks below must be true to proceed. If any single one is a "no," or if the person is unsure about any of them, the answer is to stop and ask first. Only when every check is satisfied is the document safe to upload.

**The five checks (in this order of consideration):**
1. Inside your KPMG workspace?
2. Free of NDA limits on AI tools?
3. No client-specific restriction?
4. Allowed under KPMG policy?
5. Confident it is appropriate?

**The two outcomes:**
- Clear all five checks: "Safe to upload." This is the positive outcome, in teal `#00A3A1`.
- Any "no," or any doubt on any check: "Do not upload. Ask first." This is the stop outcome, in confidential red `#B42318`.

**Required text labels (use these exactly, correctly spelled):**
- The question the tree answers: "Can this document go into Claude?"
- The five check labels, verbatim as written above.
- The positive terminal label: "Safe to upload."
- The stop terminal label: "Do not upload. Ask first."

**Your creative freedom:**

You own the design of the tree. Decide the structure, the branching, the node shapes, the flow direction, and the overall composition. Design whatever reads clearest and most elegant for this AND-logic and its two outcomes. Do not feel bound to any particular arrangement of gates, connectors, rails, or terminal positions. Make the AND-logic instantly legible: the reader should understand at a glance that every check must pass to reach "safe," and that a single "no" or any doubt routes to "ask first." Make it beautiful and make it clear.

**Canvas and brand:**
- Light or white canvas (`#FFFFFF` or a very light tone). High resolution. Generous margins.
- Brand palette, blue-led: deep blue `#00338D`, cobalt `#1E49E2`, sky `#ACEAFF`, teal `#00A3A1` for proceed, confidential red `#B42318` for stop, ink `#0B1B33`, muted ink `#5A6678`, hairline `#E3E8EF`, white `#FFFFFF`, soft tint `#F5F7FA`. Lead with blue and ink; reserve teal and red for the two outcomes so the color coding stays meaningful.
- Typography: Inter or a near-identical clean grotesque. Real, legible, correctly spelled US English. Keep labels short.
- Depth is welcome and tasteful: soft shadow, subtle layering, gentle in-palette gradients, well-made nodes. Never gaudy. Reference bar: world-class information design in the spirit of FT and Bloomberg graphics, Pentagram, and Stripe Press.

**Overall feel:**

A calm, authoritative decision aid. The reader should instantly understand: clear all five checks and it is safe to upload; fail any one, or feel any doubt, and the answer is to stop and ask first.

**Avoid:** dark or navy backgrounds, neon, purple or violet, sci-fi or circuit-board motifs, hexagon tech patterns, emoji or clip-art stock icons, childish or generic "AI infographic" looks, busy or noisy backgrounds, watermarks, and any misspelled or lorem text.
