# Diagram 1: The core loop

Diagram generation prompt (for Gemini or ChatGPT).

---

Design a genuinely well made diagram of a repeating working loop for a private equity teaching guide. It shows the reader one thing: the habit of working with Claude is a cycle you run, not a single request. This is a client-facing concept graphic for KPMG Private Equity professionals, sitting inside a light, premium, institutional web page. Aim for world-class information design, the kind of process exhibit a senior training deck would put on one page. Calm, senior, precise, and clearly made on purpose.

**The concept to visualize:**

A repeating working loop. Four moves, always in the same order, and the cycle returns to its start so the reader understands it is meant to be run again. It is not a one-way pipeline: after the fourth move, the loop comes back around to the first. The reader should also grasp that you typically run the whole loop 2 to 3 times until the output is right.

**The four moves (in this order, then back to the start):**
1. Give context: "Attach the facts and set the scene."
2. Ask precisely: "State the task, the limits, and the format."
3. Refine: "Push back in the same chat: expand, tighten, challenge."
4. Verify: "Check every figure and claim against the source."

After Verify, the loop returns to Give context. That closing return is essential to the meaning.

**Required text labels (use these exactly, correctly spelled):**
- The four move titles, verbatim: "Give context", "Ask precisely", "Refine", "Verify".
- Each move's one-line description, verbatim as written above.
- The repetition idea, expressed clearly somewhere in the composition, for example "Run the loop 2 to 3 times".

**Your creative freedom:**

You own the design. Decide the structure, the composition, the flow direction, the node shapes, and the visual metaphor for the cycle. Render it as a circle, a ring, a loop of any looping form, or any arrangement you judge clearest and most elegant, as long as the four moves read in order and the cycle visibly returns to its start. Do not feel bound to any particular arrangement of stations, positions, arcs, connectors, or a center callout. Make the looping, iterative nature instantly legible: the reader should see at a glance that this is a repeating habit, run a few times until the output is right. Make it beautiful and make it clear.

**Canvas and brand:**
- Light or white canvas (`#FFFFFF` or a very light tone). High resolution. Generous margins and quiet negative space.
- Brand palette, blue-led: deep blue `#00338D`, cobalt `#1E49E2`, sky `#ACEAFF`, teal `#00A3A1`, confidential red `#B42318`, ink `#0B1B33`, muted ink `#5A6678`, hairline `#E3E8EF`, white `#FFFFFF`, soft tint `#F5F7FA`. Lead with blue and ink. This is not a decision diagram, so use teal and red sparingly if at all.
- Typography: Inter or a near-identical clean grotesque. Real, legible, correctly spelled US English. Keep labels short.
- Depth is welcome and tasteful: soft shadow, subtle layering, gentle in-palette gradients, well-made containers. Never gaudy. Reference bar: world-class information design in the spirit of FT and Bloomberg graphics, Pentagram, and Stripe Press.

**Overall feel:**

A calm, exact, obviously iterative process diagram: dimensional and considered, with real craft in every label and connector. The reader should instantly understand that working with Claude is a loop you run 2 to 3 times until it is right.

**Avoid:** dark or navy backgrounds, neon, purple or violet, sci-fi or circuit-board motifs, hexagon tech patterns, emoji or clip-art stock icons, childish or generic "AI infographic" looks, busy or noisy backgrounds, watermarks, and any misspelled or lorem text.
