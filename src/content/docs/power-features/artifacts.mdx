Most of Claude's replies live in the flow of the conversation. Some outputs deserve their own home. An **artifact** is a standalone document Claude builds beside the chat: a memo section, a comparison table, a one-pager, the kind of thing you will keep editing rather than simply read once and scroll past.

## A document, not just a reply

When you ask Claude for something substantial and self-contained, it opens the result in a separate panel rather than burying it in the message thread. The chat stays on the left for your back-and-forth; the artifact sits on the right as a clean, focused document.

The advantage is that you can refine the document in place. Instead of scrolling up through a long conversation to find the latest version of a memo, you keep one living document on screen and shape it turn by turn.

## What belongs in an artifact

Reach for an artifact when the output is a deliverable in its own right, something you will shape and reuse rather than glance at once.

- **Memo sections**, such as investment highlights, key risks, or a transaction overview.
- **Structured tables**, like a competitor comparison or a financial summary.
- **One-pagers**, for example a screen summary a partner can read in a minute.
- **Reusable templates** you intend to apply across several targets.

A quick answer, a definition, or a short list does not need an artifact. Those belong in the chat, where they are easiest to read in context.

## Working with an artifact

<Steps>
  <Step title="Ask for a self-contained document">
    Request the deliverable directly: "Draft the key risks section as a document," or "Build a comparison table I can keep refining." Claude opens it as an artifact.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refine it by talking to the chat">
    Keep giving instructions in the conversation: "Tighten the second paragraph," "Add a row for churn," "Make the tone more partner-ready." The document updates in place.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read it in context">
    The artifact reflects everything in the conversation, so the files and figures you discussed earlier inform the draft without you re-explaining them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify, then take it out">
    Check every figure and claim against the source before the document leaves Claude. The artifact is a polished first draft, not a finished deliverable.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## A prompt that produces a clean artifact

<PromptCard title="Draft a memo section as an editable document">
You are assisting a Private Equity associate at KPMG, drafting for an investment committee.

Using the analysis and documents in this conversation, draft the "Key Risks" section of an IC memo for the target.

- Group risks under clear headings (commercial, financial, operational).
- For each, state the risk in one line, then why it matters in one or two.
- End with the three diligence questions that would most reduce uncertainty.

Produce it as a clean, self-contained document I can keep editing. Flag anything the materials do not let you assess.
</PromptCard>

## Where artifacts collect

Every artifact Claude builds is gathered in one place, the **Artifacts** entry in the left menu. That makes it easy to return to a memo section or a table you built earlier without digging through old chats. When you are working inside a deal [Project](/projects/what-projects-are), the artifacts you create stay associated with that workspace, which keeps each deal's documents together and separate.

<Callout type="confidential" title="An artifact is still your work to check">
A polished document can feel finished, which is exactly when a stray figure slips through. Treat every artifact as a first draft: verify it against the source, per [Verify Everything](/best-practices/verify-everything), before it carries the KPMG name.
</Callout>

## What to take from this

Artifacts give substantial outputs their own home, a living document beside the chat that you refine in place rather than rebuild each turn. Use them for memo sections, tables, one-pagers, and templates: anything you will shape and reuse. Drive the edits through the conversation, find your past work under the Artifacts menu, and verify the result before it leaves your desk.
