A 60-page CIM lands at 6pm and the screen is due tomorrow. Claude can turn that document into a structured one-page read in the time it takes to make a coffee. That is the real draw for Private Equity work, but the tool only earns its place when you know exactly where its strengths end and your judgment begins.

## What Claude is

Claude.ai is an **AI assistant you talk to in plain English**. You give it context (a document, a question, an instruction) and it responds with written analysis, drafts, summaries, or answers. Think of it as an exceptionally fast, well-read analyst who has read everything you give it and never tires.

For Private Equity professionals, that translates into real, everyday leverage:

- **Summarizing long documents**: distill a 60-page CIM or teaser into the points that matter in minutes.
- **Drafting**: produce a first cut of an investment memo section, an IC paper, or a management-meeting agenda.
- **Research synthesis**: pull scattered notes, articles, and filings into one coherent market view.
- **Data analysis**: interrogate a spreadsheet, sense-check figures, and surface trends worth a second look.

<Callout type="tip" title="Where it shines first">
The fastest wins are summarization and first drafts. Hand Claude something you'd otherwise read line by line, ask a precise question, and you'll feel the time saving immediately.
</Callout>

Here's a starter prompt you can adapt for almost any document on your desk:

<PromptCard title="Summarize a CIM or teaser">
You are assisting a Private Equity associate. I've attached a teaser for a potential acquisition.

Summarize it in one page using these headings:
1. Business overview (what they do, how they make money)
2. Financial highlights (revenue, EBITDA, growth, margins)
3. Investment thesis (why this could be attractive)
4. Key risks and open questions

Use bullet points. Flag anything that looks unusual or that the document is vague about.
</PromptCard>

## What Claude isn't

Claude is powerful, but it is not magic, and treating it as infallible is how mistakes reach the investment committee.

<ComparisonTable title="Strengths vs. limits" caption="Use the left column with confidence; manage the right column deliberately.">

| Claude is great at | Claude is **not** |
| --- | --- |
| Summarizing and structuring documents you provide | A live database of current market prices or news (unless connected) |
| Drafting and rewriting in a consistent tone | A replacement for your commercial judgment |
| Synthesising research you give it | Guaranteed to be correct; it can be **confidently wrong** |
| Analyzing data inside an attached file | Aware of anything you haven't told it |

</ComparisonTable>

Three limits deserve special attention:

- **It is not a live data feed.** Out of the box, Claude doesn't know today's share price or this morning's news. It works from what you give it (and, when enabled, from connectors or web search, covered later).
- **It does not replace judgment.** Claude can structure an argument; deciding whether the deal is *good* is yours.
- **It can be confidently wrong.** The output always reads fluently and authoritatively, even when a figure or fact is invented. This is the single most important thing to internalize.

<Callout type="warning" title="Always verify against the source">
Never lift a number, name, or claim from Claude into a deliverable without checking it against the original document. Fluent prose is not the same as correct prose. Verification is non-negotiable on investment work.
</Callout>

## A note on your data

The first question most Private Equity professionals ask is: *"Is it safe to put confidential material into this?"* The short answer is that on **Claude Enterprise and Team** (the plans KPMG provides) your conversations and uploaded files are **not used to train Anthropic's models**, which is exactly why these plans suit professional work.

<Callout type="confidential" title="Handle deal data deliberately">
Enterprise/Team means your data isn't used for training; it does **not** override an NDA or KPMG's data-handling rules. The full picture, workspaces, access, and what NDAs still require, lives on [Your Account & Confidential Data](/foundations/account-and-data).
</Callout>

## The takeaway

Claude is a fast, capable partner for reading, drafting, and synthesizing, used with the right context and verified against the source. Keep the judgment, and the final sign-off, firmly with you.
